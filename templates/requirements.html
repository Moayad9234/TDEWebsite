Internship Requirements

Course Information

Course: TDE 407: Field Work in Technology Education

Credits: 6 credit hours

Co-requisite: TDE 452

Restrictions: Senior Standing; Final Spring Semester

Class Meetings: Interns will meet via Zoom to discuss expectations and responsibilities.

Overview

As a capstone experience for seniors in the Technology, Engineering, and Design Education: Graphic Communication program, students are expected to complete TDE 407. This course includes a fieldwork component that connects on-the-job experience with the competencies covered in the TDE curriculum.

Justification

This course provides essential real-world experience for students entering careers in teaching, government, or industry. It addresses the gap in practical experience and offers an individualized academic component in a professional setting.

Student Learning Outcomes

Participation in the fieldwork should help students:

  • Demonstrate technical competencies in their employment field
  • Analyze real-world business practices and problems
  • Apply new technologies and exemplary practices in teaching/training
  • Synthesize workplace experiences in the graphic communications industry

Types of Field Work

Fieldwork must relate to technology, engineering, design education, or graphic communications. It must involve new roles and responsibilities (300 hours minimum) and be approved by a faculty supervisor. Paid or unpaid experiences are acceptable if meaningful and supervised.

Enrollment Steps

  1. Schedule an advising appointment the fall before your final spring semester
  2. Secure an approved internship related to your field
  3. Collaborate with your supervisor on a Memorandum of Agreement
  4. Submit the Internship Approval Form (with the MoA) by January 1st

Important Considerations

  • You must take this course during your final spring semester alongside TDE 452
  • The internship can be paid or unpaid
  • You are responsible for finding your own internship
  • 300 work hours must be completed by the end of the semester
  • You are responsible for your own transportation to the internship site
